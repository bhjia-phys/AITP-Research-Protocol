# Roadmap: v1.36 L0 L1 Source Intelligence And Physicist-Grade Intake

## Overview

`v1.36` is shipped.

It closed the front-side research-substrate gap that remained after `v1.35` by
strengthening source identity, citation/source-neighbor intelligence,
assumption/regime-aware intake, contradiction/notation-aware intake honesty,
and runtime/topic read-path exposure of those signals.

## Phases

- [x] **Phase 42: Source Identity And Citation Graph**
  - Goal: strengthen `L0` source identity and citation/source-neighbor signals.
  - Outcome: durable source identity and richer citation/source-neighbor signals
    now land in the runtime stack.

- [x] **Phase 43: Assumption And Regime Intake**
  - Goal: strengthen `L1` intake with source-backed assumption, regime, and
    reading-depth structure.
  - Outcome: `l1_source_intake` now survives source distillation, contract
    materialization, and runtime projection.

- [x] **Phase 44: Contradiction And Notation Alignment**
  - Goal: surface contradiction candidates and notation-alignment tension during
    intake.
  - Outcome: conflict-aware `l1_source_intake` now persists contradiction and
    notation-tension signals durably.

- [x] **Phase 45: Runtime Source-Intelligence Read Path**
  - Goal: expose stronger `L0/L1` intelligence in runtime/topic read paths.
  - Outcome: runtime bundle, topic status, dashboard, and runtime protocol note
    now expose durable `source_intelligence` plus conflict-aware intake data.

## Key Accomplishments

1. Rebuilt the rejected `v1.37-v1.42` chain on a clean remediation branch
   anchored at trusted baseline `bd98af5`.
2. Added durable source identity plus citation/source-neighbor signals that can
   be reused across related topics.
3. Materialized source-backed `L1` intake for assumptions, regimes, reading
   depth, contradiction candidates, and notation-alignment tension.
4. Exposed the stronger `L0/L1` intelligence through runtime/topic read paths
   instead of hiding it in internal-only artifacts.
5. Brought `topic_shell_support.py` and `runtime_bundle_support.py` back under
   maintainability-watch budgets while keeping the full knowledge-hub suite
   green.
